Problem Solving

Blocked on a simple problem:

I ran into a few simple problems during this task. One of them occurred while manipulating objects. The task required adding Terah's children to a new key along with their respective values. Initially, I attempted to add them directly under the children key. However, upon checking, an error emerged. So, I had to revisit the instructions and the expected outcome from the terminal, breaking down what was being asked. I realized that I needed to assign each of the children's names under terah.children with their own property and value.

Throughout the process, I felt somewhat frustrated and found myself second-guessing what I was doing wrong. However, once I pinpointed my mistake, a sense of relief washed over me, and I understood what needed to happen. I learned the importance of calming down, slowing down, and breaking the problem into smaller parts. I also realized that error messages are helpful in identifying where things went wrong and understanding what the code expects.

Elegantly solved a problem

A problem I elegantly solved was during Gradebook Question 3, which involved adding a new property with a key 'testScores'. Initially, I attempted to directly input the scores into the student's object literals along with the corresponding array positions of their scores. However, I encountered an error as I wasn't supposed to do that. So, I had to review the question and see what it was asking for. It turned out that I needed to add a new property and its value.

The correction transformed gradebook.Joseph = {score[0]} into gradebook.Joseph.testScore = (scores[0]). It felt really satisfying once I figured it out. Again, it reinforced the importance of looking at things properly and breaking them down.

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time?

During the challenge, I tend to tackle the problem on my own because I lack the confidence to ask for help from others. Perhaps it's due to my own thinking that I might not be doing it right and wouldn't want to waste anyone's time. Or sometimes, I check if someone else has raised a similar question and see if it aligns with where I'm at. However, next time, I need to try approaching it differently by explaining my question properly, detailing where I'm stuck with my work, and asking for help in the group chats.
